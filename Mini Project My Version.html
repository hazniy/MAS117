Introduction

This project simulates a random walk by Annie the ant on a pentagon. Annie starts at vertex 0 and takes seven random steps, moving either clockwise or counterclockwise with equal probability.

Objectives

The goal is to simulate Annie's random walk with:

  1. Fixed values for vertices (5) and steps (7).

  2. An extended version where the user specifies the number of vertices and steps.

Python Code: Fixed Values

A Python program

Here, the program simulates 128 random walks and shows the frequency of each final vertex. The fixed values are 5 vertices and 7 steps.

Effects of the Number of Steps and Vertices

Number of Steps Number of Vertices
More steps increase the likelihood of reaching different vertices. Increasing the vertices spreads the distribution.
Example:
  • With fewer steps (e.g., 3 steps), results are concentrated near the starting point.
  • With more steps (e.g., 10 steps), resultrs are more spread out.
Example:
  • With fewer vertices (e.g., 3 vertices), the final positions are concentrated.
  • With more vertices (e.g., 10 vertices), the final positions are more varied.

The number of steps and vertices both play a big role in shaping where Annie ends up. With more vertices, her final positions are spread out across a larger range. Similarly, taking more steps increases the chances of her exploring more of the vertices along the way.
